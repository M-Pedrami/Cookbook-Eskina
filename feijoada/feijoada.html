The original feijoada

Feijoada, a soulful Brazilian dish, is a heartwarming black bean stew brimming with various succulent cuts of pork.

Picture tender meats, smoky sausage, and hearty black beans simmering together, creating a symphony of flavors. Imagine the warm, aromatic smells of garlic, onions, and smoked meats wafting through the air, mingling with the earthy scent of beans. The deep, rich gravy is a visual treat, contrasting against the zesty orange slices, bright green kale, and fluffy white rice served alongside.

It's a feast for the senses, capturing the diverse flavors and textures of Brazil in every mouthful.

Ingredients

  1. 500g dry black beans
  2. 500g pork shoulder, cut into chunks
  3. 200g smoked sausage, sliced
  4. 200g bacon, diced
  5. 2 large onions, chopped
  6. 5 cloves of garlic, minced
  7. 2 bay leaves
  8. Salt and pepper to taste
  9. Orange slices, for garnish
For the sides:
  1. 5 cups cooked white rice
  2. 5 cups sauteed collard greens or kale

Steps

  1. Soak the black beans overnight in a large bowl with plenty of water.
  2. In a large pot, cook the bacon over medium heat until crispy.
  3. Add the pork shoulder and smoked sausage to the pot. Cook until nicely browned.
  4. Add in the onions, garlic, and bay leaves. Cook until the onions are translucent.
  5. Drain the beans and add them to the pot. Add enough water to cover the ingredients by about 2 inches.
  6. Season to taste with salt and pepper.
  7. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for about 4 hours, or until the beans are tender and the flavors are well combined.
  8. Serve hot with a side of rice and sauteed greens, garnished with orange slices.
